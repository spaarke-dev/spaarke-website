## Card Summary / Why This Matters

> Legal departments are data-rich and insight-poor. They manage billions in spend, oversee critical risk, and operate at the intersection of every business function — yet most still run on fragmented tools, tribal knowledge, and reactive workflows. Legal Operations Intelligence is the emerging discipline that changes this. It is the systematic application of unified data, institutional memory, and inference to transform how legal departments operate and decide.

## Structure

### 1. Why This Matters (~150 words)
The gap between legal's strategic importance and its operational sophistication. Frame as a business problem, not a technology problem.

### 2. The Problem: Smart People, Fragmented Systems (~300 words)
Paint the current state. ELM, CLM, e-billing, spreadsheets, email. Data everywhere, insight nowhere. Institutional knowledge lives in people's heads. Every matter starts from zero. The average legal department uses 7+ disconnected systems. No other function in the enterprise operates this way.

### 3. What Legal Operations Intelligence Is (~400 words)
Define LOI as a category. It is not a tool — it is a discipline and a platform approach. Introduce the IQ Stack:
- **Data**: Capture how work gets done — matter data, spend data, workflow data, document data
- **Memory**: Retain organizational context — decisions, rationale, patterns, precedent
- **Inference**: Turn signals into decisions — analytics, prediction, recommendation

Distinguish from adjacent categories:
- **Legal tech** = tools (CLM, e-billing, etc.)
- **Legal analytics** = dashboards and reports
- **AI for lawyers** = document review, contract analysis
- **LOI** = the operating intelligence layer across all of these

### 4. Why Now (~300 words)
Three converging forces:
1. **AI is forcing every department to get its data house in order.** You cannot apply intelligence to chaos. AI amplifies what you have — if your data is fragmented, AI gives you fragmented answers.
2. **Legal spend is under unprecedented scrutiny.** CFOs are asking harder questions. "We don't know" is no longer an acceptable answer about a $20B+ annual category.
3. **The next generation of legal ops leaders thinks in systems, not tools.** They have seen what unified data platforms do for marketing, sales, and finance. They expect the same for legal.

### 5. What This Means for Your Organization (~300 words)
Concrete outcomes LOI enables:
- Predictive spend management (forecast before invoices arrive)
- Preserved institutional knowledge (context survives turnover)
- Context-aware workflows (routing that adapts, not just automates)
- Cross-functional visibility (legal data connected to business outcomes)
