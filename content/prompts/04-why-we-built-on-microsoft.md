## Purpose

Establish Spaarke's Microsoft-first positioning as a strategic advantage, not just a technology choice. This is where the series pivots from "what is LOI" to "how Spaarke delivers it." The Microsoft ecosystem is the foundation — this article explains why that matters to legal leaders.

## Card Summary / Why This Matters

> When we chose to build Spaarke on the Microsoft ecosystem, it was not a technology decision — it was a strategic one. More than 85% of Fortune 500 companies run on Microsoft 365. Legal departments already live in Outlook, Teams, SharePoint, and Word. Building on this foundation means Spaarke works where your people already work, inside the security and compliance boundaries your organization already trusts.

## Structure

### 1. Why This Matters (~150 words)
Platform choice is not a technical detail — it determines your data boundaries, your AI capabilities, and your total cost of ownership. For legal departments handling the most sensitive information in the organization, the platform decision is a governance decision. Reference the LOI framework from Article 1.

### 2. Where Legal Work Already Lives (~250 words)
Most legal work happens in M365: drafting in Word, communicating in Outlook/Teams, storing in SharePoint. Any legal ops platform that ignores this reality creates friction and fragmentation. Spaarke meets teams where they already are.

Key points:
- Contracts drafted in Word. Communications in Outlook and Teams. Documents in SharePoint.
- Adding a platform outside this ecosystem means duplicate logins, copy-paste workflows, and data fragmentation
- Spaarke runs natively within M365 — no context switching, no data duplication, no parallel universe

### 3. The Enterprise Trust Factor (~250 words)
Microsoft's compliance certifications, security infrastructure, and enterprise agreements. Legal departments handle the most sensitive information in the organization — privileged communications, litigation strategy, M&A intelligence, regulatory filings.

Key points:
- Microsoft's compliance portfolio: SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA, FedRAMP, GDPR
- Enterprise agreements already in place — procurement, vendor risk assessment, and security review have been done
- Building on Microsoft means inheriting a security posture validated by the most regulated industries
- Your CISO already trusts Microsoft. That trust extends to platforms built within the ecosystem.

### 4. Tenant Dedicated Deployment (~300 words)
**Introduce this concept** — first use in the series. Spaarke deploys within the customer's own Microsoft 365 tenant using Power Platform. This is the modern equivalent of on-premises.

Key points:
- Your data never leaves your environment
- Your IT team controls access through existing Entra ID / Azure AD
- Your compliance policies (DLP, retention, classification) apply automatically
- This is fundamentally different from multi-tenant SaaS platforms that host your data on their infrastructure
- Name it: "Tenant Dedicated Deployment" — coined to describe this approach
- Deeper exploration in Article 6

### 5. The Copilot Advantage (~200 words)
More than 50% of legal organizations are now using Microsoft Copilot as their AI tool. Spaarke's AI is built to work within the M365 Copilot plane.

Key points:
- Not a separate AI silo — intelligence that flows through the tools your team already uses
- Copilot grounded in Spaarke's structured legal data produces dramatically better results than generic Copilot
- Data boundaries maintained — AI operates within your tenant, governed by your policies
- Brief introduction here; expanded in Article 8
