## Card Summary / Why This Matters

> Every legal department falls somewhere on a spectrum — from purely reactive (putting out fires) to genuinely predictive (anticipating and preventing them). Most know they want to be further along but lack a clear framework for understanding where they are and what to do next. The Legal Operations Intelligence Maturity Model provides that framework.

## Structure

### 1. Why This Matters (~150 words)
You cannot improve what you cannot assess. This model gives legal ops leaders a common language for their current state and a roadmap forward. Reference the LOI framework (Article 1) and IQ Stack (Article 2) — this model maps progression through those concepts.

### 2. The Five Levels (~800 words, ~160 each)
Define each level with characteristics, typical tools, and outcomes:

**Level 1: Ad Hoc**
- Spreadsheets, email, tribal knowledge. No system of record.
- Work is reactive — every request starts from scratch
- No visibility into spend, volume, or outcomes
- Knowledge lives entirely in people's heads

**Level 2: Organized**
- Point solutions in place (ELM, CLM, e-billing). Data exists but is siloed.
- Some process standardization, but systems do not talk to each other
- Reporting is possible but manual and backward-looking
- Beginning of the Data layer, but fragmented

**Level 3: Integrated**
- Systems connected. Unified data layer emerging. Reporting is possible.
- Cross-system visibility beginning to form
- Process consistency improving; workflows becoming repeatable
- Data layer of the IQ Stack is taking shape

**Level 4: Intelligent**
- Operational memory captured. Pattern recognition active. Workflows adapt.
- Decisions are informed by historical context, not just current data
- Memory layer active — institutional knowledge is being retained systematically
- Early inference: recommendations, anomaly detection, trend analysis

**Level 5: Predictive**
- Full LOI. Inference drives decisions. The organization learns continuously.
- AI grounded in organizational data and memory
- Predictive spend management, resource planning, outcome forecasting
- The flywheel from Article 2 is fully operational
- Legal operates as a strategic function, not a cost center

### 3. How to Assess Your Level (~300 words)
Practical diagnostic questions for each level transition:
- "Can you answer these five questions about your legal spend right now?" (Level 1→2)
- "Can you get a single view across matters, spend, and documents without combining spreadsheets?" (Level 2→3)
- "When a key team member leaves, what happens to their knowledge?" (Level 3→4)
- "Can your system recommend actions based on historical patterns?" (Level 4→5)

Frame as a self-assessment the reader can do immediately. Make it concrete enough to be uncomfortable — most readers will recognize they are at Level 1 or 2.

### 4. Moving Up: It Is Not About Buying More Tools (~200 words)
The maturity path is about architecture and discipline, not adding more point solutions. More tools at Level 2 keeps you at Level 2 — with more data silos. Progression requires the IQ Stack architecture (Article 2): unify data first, then build memory, then enable inference. Each level maps to a deeper engagement with the IQ Stack.
