## Purpose

Deep dive into Spaarke's foundational architecture framework. Makes the abstract concept from Article 1 concrete and memorable. This article is the architectural companion to the manifesto — where Article 1 says "what," this article says "how."

## Card Summary / Why This Matters

> Most legal technology solves one problem at a time. A contract tool here, a billing system there, a workflow app in between. The IQ Stack is a different approach — a three-layer architecture designed to make your entire legal operation smarter over time. Data captures how work actually gets done. Memory retains context so your organization never starts from zero. Inference turns accumulated signals into actionable decisions.

## Structure

### 1. Why This Matters (~150 words)
Point solutions create data silos. The IQ Stack is an architecture for compounding intelligence. As we defined in "What is Legal Operations Intelligence?" — LOI requires a unified platform approach. The IQ Stack is that architecture.

### 2. Layer 1: Data — Capture How Work Gets Done (~350 words)
Unified matter data, spend data, workflow data, document data. Not just storage — structured capture across the full matter lifecycle.

Key points:
- Data unification is the foundation; without it, memory and inference have nothing to work with
- Current state: data scattered across ELM, CLM, e-billing, email, spreadsheets, SharePoint
- The IQ Stack's Data layer captures across all these touchpoints into a unified model
- Reference how this breaks down the silos described in Article 1

### 3. Layer 2: Memory — Retain What Your Organization Knows (~350 words)
This is the most differentiated concept. Operational memory = the decisions, rationale, context, and patterns that usually live in people's heads or buried in email threads.

Key points:
- Why this is different from document storage: documents capture *what* was decided; memory captures *why*, under what constraints, with what tradeoffs
- Concrete examples: why a specific outside counsel was chosen, what negotiation leverage worked with a counterparty, how a particular matter type typically unfolds
- Business impact: turnover resilience, consistency, faster onboarding, higher quality decisions
- Memory compounds over time — the organization gets smarter with every matter

### 4. Layer 3: Inference — Turn Signals into Decisions (~350 words)
Pattern recognition, predictive analytics, recommendations. Not AI for AI's sake — intelligence grounded in your organization's own data and memory.

Key points:
- Inference is only as good as the data and memory beneath it
- Examples: "Based on 200 similar matters, this one will likely cost X and take Y months." "This contract clause was rejected in 3 of your last 5 negotiations with this counterparty."
- Briefly introduce the M365 Copilot integration angle — Spaarke's Inference layer works within the Copilot plane, meaning AI that operates inside your organizational boundary
- Expanded in a future article (forward tease to Article 8)

### 5. How the Layers Compound (~200 words)
The real power: each layer makes the others more valuable over time.
- More data improves memory (richer context to capture)
- Richer memory improves inference (better grounding for AI)
- Better inference surfaces which data to capture next (the system identifies its own gaps)

This is a flywheel, not a stack of features. Frame this as the fundamental difference between LOI and bolting together point solutions.
