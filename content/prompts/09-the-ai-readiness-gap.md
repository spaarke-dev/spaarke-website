## Purpose

Creates urgency. Positions data readiness as the prerequisite for AI success — not the AI tools themselves. This article is the bridge between the AI articles (7, 8) and the operational articles (10-13). It argues: you cannot get AI right until you get your operations right.

## Card Summary / Why This Matters

> Most legal departments are not ready for AI. Not because the technology is immature — but because their data is. AI is only as good as the information it can access, and most legal operations run on fragmented systems, inconsistent data, and undocumented processes. The readiness gap is not a technology problem. It is an operations problem.

## Structure

### 1. Why This Matters (~150 words)
Everyone is talking about AI for legal. Few are talking about whether legal departments have the operational foundation to actually benefit from it. The conversation has jumped from "should we use AI?" to "which AI tool?" without stopping at "are we ready?" Reference the maturity model (Article 3) — most organizations at Levels 1-2 are not ready.

### 2. The Readiness Gap (~300 words)
Define the readiness gap: the distance between where your legal data and processes are today and where they need to be for AI to deliver real value.

Symptoms:
- Matter data is inconsistent — different people enter the same information differently (or not at all)
- No central repository — documents in SharePoint, email, local drives, and vendor portals
- Decisions are not captured — the "why" behind actions lives in someone's head or a buried email
- Spend data lives in spreadsheets — invoices are processed but not analyzed
- Workflows are in email — routing, approvals, and escalations happen informally

The uncomfortable truth: pointing AI at this landscape does not produce intelligence — it produces confident-sounding noise. Reference "garbage in, garbage out" but elevate it: with AI, it is "garbage in, *authoritative-sounding* garbage out" — which is worse.

### 3. Why Tools Alone Will Not Close It (~250 words)
Buying an AI tool and pointing it at messy data produces messy output. The organizations getting real value from AI are the ones that first invested in their data and operational architecture.

Key points:
- An AI tool layered on fragmented data gives you fragmented answers — faster
- The tool is not the bottleneck; the data architecture is
- More tools at Level 2 (maturity model, Article 3) keeps you at Level 2
- Connect to the LOI Maturity Model: readiness corresponds to Level 3+ on the maturity scale

### 4. Closing the Gap: An LOI Approach (~300 words)
The IQ Stack (Article 2) sequence is designed to close this gap:

1. **Start with Data** — Unify matter, spend, and document data into a single structured platform. This is not a data migration project — it is changing how data is captured going forward.

2. **Build Memory** — As work flows through the unified platform, context accumulates naturally. Decisions, outcomes, rationale, and patterns become part of the organizational record.

3. **Then enable Inference** — AI that draws on structured, contextual, organization-specific data produces genuinely useful output. This is the difference between Copilot that gives generic answers and Copilot that gives *your* answers.

This sequence is not accidental — each layer prepares for the next. Skipping to Inference without Data and Memory is why most legal AI pilots underperform.

### 5. Start Here (~200 words)
Practical first steps — make this actionable:
- **Audit your data landscape**: Where does your legal data live today? How many systems? How connected?
- **Identify the three highest-value data sources**: What data, if unified, would create the most immediate insight?
- **Unify them**: Not with integrations — with a platform that captures at the source
- **Then add intelligence**: Once your Data layer is clean, Memory and Inference become possible

Position Spaarke as the platform designed for this progression — but frame it as the logical answer, not a pitch.
